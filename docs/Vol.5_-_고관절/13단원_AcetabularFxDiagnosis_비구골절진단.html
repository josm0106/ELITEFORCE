1) Anatomy

(1) anterior column - iliac crest - anterior half of the iliac crest, the iliac spines, the anterior half of the acetabulum, and the ischium (2) posterior column - ischial tuberosity부터 posteroinferior일 수 있다는 obturator foramen과 ischium 사이부터 시작 - ischial spine, the posterior half of the acetabulum, and the dense bone forming the sciatic notch

(3) acetabulum 기능적 해부 중요

⊕ ilium dome - 둔근 부위와 떨어진 기능적 기능 중요 - 자체 골밀도 훨씬더 - anatomical representation중요 ⊕ pelvic brim - 골밀도 역할 상체 골절 - 치골과 박리되는 역할, 몸준족할 때는 손 중하는 위치 ⊕ peal acetabulum - 가장 안정적 골절까지 가장 중요한 부위 ⊕ quadrilateral surface - flat pelvis forming the lateral border of the true pelvic cavity and thus lying between the acetabulum ⊕ The iliopectineal eminence - prominence in the anterior column that lies directly over the femoral head - Both the quadrilateral surface and the iliopectineal eminence are thin and adjacent to the femoral head, limiting the types of fixation that can be used in these regions

2) Radiographic evaluation

(1) landmark of standard AP view

⊕ ilioischial line - beginning at greater sciatic notch of ilium and extending down to public tubercle ⊕ iliopubic line - formed by posterior four-fifths of quadrilateral surface of ilium ⊕ anterior acetabular rim - including anterior termination of iliopubic line ⊕ posterior acetabular rim - edge of anterior lip of acetabulum ⊕ edge of posterior lip of acetabulum ⊕ obturator or internal oblique view ⊕ U자형 iliopubis ⊕ & Acetabular view 시에 보기 ⊕ spur sign ⊕ pect. lip ⊕ obturator foramen ⊕ III ant. column (iliopubic column) ⊕ IV iliopectineal line ⊕ VI ant. roof arc ⊕ VII spur sign (both column fx 아닐)

(2) axial CT image

⊕ measuring Fs lines and Ps of ant. and post. wall in sagittal plane ⊕ ant. and post. column Ps - extends through quadrilateral surface into obturator foramen with a more coronal orientation

(1) simple fx A : post. wall fx B : post. column fx C : ant. wall fx D : ant. column fx E : transverse fx (2) complex fx F : post. column & post wall fx G : transverse & post. wall fx H : T-shaped fx I : ant. column & post. hemitransverse fx J : complete both-column fx
