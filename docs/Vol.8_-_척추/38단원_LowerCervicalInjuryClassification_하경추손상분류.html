Lower cervical injury classification (C3-7)

하경추 손상 분류

교과서 내용 정리

1) 하경추 손상 분류 개요 (Lower Cervical Injury Classification C3-7)

(1) 분류 시스템

• 기본 분류체계: Check the diagnosis of clinical instability in lower cervical spine

• 주요 분류 기준

① Stability: defined as ability to function and maintain intact neurologic and visceral structures

② Check instability: most useful for documenting clinical instability

③ Radiographic findings: aid diagnosis when present

④ Anterolateral subluxation stage: determines initial treatment course

(2) Soft Tissue Injury (연부조직 손상)

• 기본 병태생리

① Unilateral loss of function is most useful for documenting clinical instability in lower cervical spine. It is uncommon to have continuous unilateral loss associated with neurologic injury. For this reason, direct surgical should not be performed on the lesion, except if there is significant neurologic loss.

2) 주요 분류체계

(1) Compressive Flexion Injury (CF)

• CF Stage I

① Mild compression of anterior vertebral body

② Anterior margin of disc space remains intact

③No posterior ligament disruption

• CF Stage II

① Bilateral lamina fractures, can be multiple levels

• CF Stage III

① Bilateral lamina, facet, pedicle fractures without vertebral body displacement. Although admittedly "hypothetical"— having not been encountered in their review, they may be described as a floating lateral mass fracture

• CF Stage IV

① As for CF stage 3, with partial anterior vertebral body displacement

• CF Stage V

① As for CF stage 3, with 100% anterior vertebral body displacement

(2) Distractive Flexion Injury (DF)

• DF Stage I

① Abnormal widening of the disc space, may or may not be avulsion fractures of the anterior vertebral body margin, no posterior translation

• DF Stage II

① DF stage 1 plus posterior translation

(3) Distractive Extension Injury (DE)

• DE Stage I

① Abnormal widening of disc space, may or may not be avulsion fractures of anterior vertebral body margin, no posterior translation

• DE Stage II

① DE stage 1 plus posterior translation

(4) Lateral Flexion Injury (LF)

• LF Stage I

① Unilateral uncovertebral vertebral fracture or asymmetric vertebral body compression

• LF Stage II

① Vertebral body or posterior arch fractures with lateral translation or unilateral facet gapping, coronal angular deformity is noted on an AP radiograph

(5) Vertical Compression Injury (VC)

• VC Stage I

① Stable compression fracture

• VC Stage II

① Superior end-plate fractures, sometimes with retropulsion of bone into the spinal canal. Progressive kyphosis may occur.

• VC Stage III

① Failure of the vertebral body, superior compression, burst fractures

② Has been subsequently described by other authors as a "exploded disc fracture"

(6) Vertical Compression with Posterior Element Injury (CE)

• CE Stage 1

① Vertical compression with disruption of posterior ligaments

② Posterior element injury present

• CE Stage 2

① Bilateral lamina fractures, can be multiple levels

• CE Stage 3

① Bilateral lamina, facet, pedicle fractures without vertebral body displacement

• CE Stage 4

① As for CE stage 3, with partial anterior vertebral body displacement

• CE Stage 5

① As for CE stage 3, with 100% anterior vertebral body displacement

3) Kim's Solution

DF Stage I

- Facet subluxation, gapping of the spinous process ligaments, indicating failure of the PLC with or without some blunting of anterosuperior vertebral body (like CF stage I)

DF I & 다른채 치료

- 정복적 이석정치 경추 경추 보조기 또는 halo vest 8-12주

- flexion-extension view에서 불안정성 보이면 정골 수술적 고정 등을 고려

4) Subaxial Injury Classification (SLIC) Scale

MORPHOLOGY POINTS

--------------------

No abnormality 0

Compression + burst 1+1 = 2

Distraction (e.g., facet perch, hyperextension) 3

Rotation/translation injury (e.g., facet dislocation, unstable teardrop, or advanced stage flexion compression injury) 4

DISCOLIGAMENTOUS COMPLEX

Intact 0

Indeterminate (e.g., isolated interspinous widening, MRI signal change only) 1

Disrupted (e.g., widening of anterior disc space, facet perch, or dislocation) 2

NEUROLOGICAL STATUS

Intact 0

Root injury 1

Complete cord injury 2

Incomplete cord injury 3

Continuous cord compression (neuro-modifier in the sitting of a neurological deficit) +1
